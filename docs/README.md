# OctoAcme Project Management Documentation

## Overview of Project Management Processes

OctoAcme operates a **customer-first, iterative project management framework** that emphasizes clear ownership, data-driven decisions, and psychological safety across all cross-functional projects. The organization follows a structured five-stage lifecycle: Initiation (validating business need and success metrics), Planning (breaking work into shippable increments), Execution (building and testing), Release (deploying to production), and Retrospective (capturing learnings). Projects are governed by key artifacts including Project Charters, roadmaps, release plans, sprint backlogs, and risk registers. The core principles prioritize delivering small, testable increments while maintaining measurable outcomes and evidence-based decision-making.

**OctoAcme's organizational structure defines three primary personas**: Project Managers who coordinate delivery, manage schedules, risks, and stakeholder communications; Product Managers who define product vision, prioritize backlogs, and measure success through user research and metrics; and Developers who implement features, write tests, participate in design reviews, and help identify technical risks. This clear separation of roles ensures accountability and smooth collaboration. Weekly syncs between PM and Product Manager, twice-weekly standups for delivery teams, and monthly stakeholder updates form the backbone of the organizational communication cadence.

**Communication and risk management are deeply embedded** throughout OctoAcme's processes. The organization maintains a structured Risk Register (tracking ID, description, impact, likelihood, owner, and mitigation) that is updated weekly during project syncs and reviewed continuously during execution. An escalation path moves issues from team-level (daily standups) through PM and Product Lead levels to sponsor-level escalations for business-impacting concerns. Weekly status updates follow a consistent template covering progress, next steps, risks/blockers, and required decisions, ensuring transparency across all stakeholder groups.

**Quality assurance and continuous improvement are woven into every phase of execution and release**. During the Execution & Tracking phase, teams follow a pull request workflow with small PRs (≤400 lines), automated CI testing, and mandatory code reviews before merging. Quality practices include unit tests, integration tests, end-to-end smoke tests for critical flows, and manual QA for feature acceptance. Release processes require passing CI, security scans, drafted release notes, and prepared rollback plans before deployment. Finally, OctoAcme conducts structured retrospectives after each sprint, release, or milestone (timebox 45–75 minutes) to identify what went well, what could improve, and prioritize 2–3 actionable improvement items with clear owners and timelines, embedding continuous learning into the organization's culture.
